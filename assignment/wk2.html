Analysis of HP Fan Fiction character topics

Hello! In today's analysis we begin by trying to understand which characters are most commonly mentioned in HP(Harry Potter) fan fiction to juxtapose it to the original J.K. Rowling HP books. We're going to also analyze how these characters interact and are portrayed in comparison to the originals. This will allow us to make interpretations on which characters are most favored by the community and for what reasons.

To analyze word occurances, we're going to be using the AntConc ngram feature and feed it a giant list of names gathered from harry potter and sources online. Let's get to it...

But why is Severus Snape more popular, to find out lets see how he's used in context of the fan fiction Corpus.

So, from a look of concordance with the words Grandpa and Snape, we see that most of these occurances of portraying Snape as an endearing grandfather is by DramaGirl. Which from a quick look at concordance with Snape and Brenda, we see Dramagirl's plot is meant to be a fictional spin-off of Harry Potter where Harry Potter is now a girl named Brenda Potter. This is verified through a quick look at the corpus mentioning a "Ms. Potter." We also see a strong relationship built on trust between Dumbledore and Snape, which matches the originals. From this, we see Snape as a loving and heartbroken man. From him being portrayed as a loving grandpa, taking care of Ms. Potter to spying for Dumbledore in 'Witchdragon_dragonheart'. This could potentially mean that fan fiction writers resonated with the loss of Snape's beloved Lily in the originals so much that they decided to portray his chivalrous, protaganistic behavior in their works. One might argue that specific fan fiction might mention Severus too much and it is not an accurate representation of the community. So the last slide contains a Concordance plot to investigate the distribution of his name. The plot shows that 7 of the 12 texts mentioned Snape at least once, which is more than half of the texts in our dictionary. This shows that the distribution is quite spread out across texts and not skewed drastically due to simply one text.

In conclusion, from a distant reading and analysis of a selection of Harry Potter FF texts, we conclude that Snape is more favored in the community than Rowling might've expected. We've discussed potential reasons for this. The main being that the community had included him in their stories to finally give a happy ending to Severus Snape, in contrast to the original, where he dies a heartbroken man while fighting his lover's killer- Lord Voldemort.
