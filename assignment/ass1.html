Analysis of PLOS ONE "Military" Corpus

Hello, today we'll generating a Corpus containing any occurances of the word military, then running an analysis on what specific topics are refered to in the text.

We first generate the corpora using AntCorGen and query for any occurances of the word "military" as follows:

To analyze word occurances, we're going to be using the Voyant wordcloud feature to get a general understanding of the text, we will then see in what contexts they're used to get an idea of the topics dicussed. Let's get to it...

Getting context

We see some interesting and frequent words used are: military, risk, health, study. From this we assume the Corpus contains a lot of studies discussing health risks of working in the Defense industry. To verify this we need some more context, so we run a concordance on the word risk. Lets also run a concordance on the word military to understand the corpus better.

Concordance on the word Military
Concordance on the word Risk

As expected we see the word risk being used to identify health impact on military troops, through a closer look we see a lot of articles discussing respiratory diseases and tuberculosis, possibly due to smoking addiction across soldiers. We also notice something very interesting from concordance on the word Military, we see a lot of mention of specific countries and regions. Which contries are mentioned most frequently and why? We answer this through generating a word list that searches for a set of specific words.

Which militaries and why?

We download a set of countries and their acronyms from here. We use this data to generate a set of search terms that we put in the advanced option of our clusters tool and run a cluster analysis to determine which phrases or countries are more frequently mentioned. It is likely that the most mentioned contries would be those with large militaries are those that have partaken in popular wars.

We immidiately realize that acronyms seem to be top, which is abnormal. But through running a concordance on some of the most frequently used acronyms we notice that they are being used in the context of units. An example being CI, which is being used as the unit Curie and not the country Côte d'Ivoire in all cases. As such, lets ignore units that might be mistaken for countries and only look at country phrases when ranking the most mentioned countries. With this in mind we recognize the most frequently mentioned countries are China, USA, Iraq, Peru, and Thailand in that order from highest to lowest. Why are these countries mentioned frequently? Peru especially stands out as it hasn't had a war since 1995 and is ranked 50 in Military Strength; perhaps Peru is not mentioned in the context of its military but something else. To investigate we run a concordance on the word Peru. We also investigate why China is mentioned more than the US and on what specific topics through running a concordance on the word China.

From the concordance we determine China and Peru are mentioned almost entirely due to viral outbreaks in their regions. One of the main reason Peru is the most frequently mentioned is because Peru is currently dealing with an epidemic cased by a mosquito-borne viral disease known as Dengue. An example of this is in a paper that discusses preventative measures such as installing insecticide treated curtains in Peruvian homes, we discover this through a combination of concordance and file view. Running a concordance for Peru in the context of the word military reveals that articles are solely discuss disease outbreaks and their affect on military troops stationed in Jungles and not a comparison of world powers. This is mainly due to the fact a large majority of articles published in he PLOS journals are in Epidemiology and Medicine according to their FAQ.

In conclusion, we discovered the corpus discusses the affects of mostly tropical diseases in certain regions and their affect on military personel stationed in those regions. This can be expected considering the topics published in the PLOS one journals. The reason China and Peru are frequently mentioned in these articles could be simply due to the fact that there is a larger spread of disease in those regions.
