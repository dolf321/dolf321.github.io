Response 4: Go back to the original podcast featuring Adwetewa-Badu and evaluate what you have learned this semester.

Throughout the RLAC course, I have learned and experienced a variety of methods and techniques for understanding the cultural, ethnic, and social contexts of large corpora. One of the key tools we studied was stylometry, which involves the use of statistical analysis to identify the unique writing style of an author. This can be useful for identifying multiple authors within a single corpus or for detecting changes in an author's style over time. In addition to stylometry, we also learned about other techniques such as word embeddings and distinctive words. Word embeddings involve creating a mathematical representation of a word based on its context within a corpus, while distinctive words involve identifying the words that are most unique to a particular corpus or subset of a corpus. Both of these techniques can be useful for understanding the content and themes of a corpus in a more quantitative and analytical manner. Another important concept we studied was sentiment analysis, which involves using natural language processing techniques to identify the overall sentiment of a corpus. This can be useful for understanding the emotional tone of a corpus and for identifying any shifts in sentiment over time. One of the key contrasts we learned about was the difference between close and distant reading. Close reading involves meticulously reading a text from left to right in order to uncover deep connotations and meaning. This is often used in literature analysis, where the goal is to understand the intricacies of a single text. Distant reading, on the other hand, involves a broader reading of a corpus in order to gain a more general understanding of its content and themes. Distant reading can be useful for identifying patterns and trends within a corpus, but it is not a substitute for close reading. Throughout the course, I have come to understand that close and distant reading are best used together in order to support a hypothesis. By doing a distant reading of a corpus to identify interesting phrases or elements, we can then do a close reading around these words or phrases to gain a deeper understanding of their context and meaning.
